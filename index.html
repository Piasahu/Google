Public challenge

Turn workforce development into an intelligent public service

The strongest story for Google.org is not simply “education with AI.” It is a government innovation platform that uses AI to improve how citizens are assessed, guided, trained, and connected to real economic opportunity.

What is broken today

Public training programs often operate with static catalogs, manual intake, and weak feedback loops from employers. Citizens are treated as cohorts, not individuals, so skills gaps remain hidden and outcomes are hard to verify.

Nearshoring mismatch Tourism growth pressure Low personalization

What this pilot changes

A single conversational system diagnoses learner readiness, recommends a pathway, supports tutoring, captures evidence of progress, and returns anonymized workforce signals to government decision makers in one continuous loop.

LLM-guided intake Adaptive tutoring Policy intelligence

Technical design

From validated pilot to five-agent public-service stack

The pilot can stay simple enough to launch quickly, while still being intentionally modular.

Single-LLM orchestration pilot

Our AI stack handles intake, diagnosis, pathway recommendation, and adaptive tutoring inside one conversational flow. State rules, learner profiles, course metadata, and employer signals are retrieved from a structured data layer so the pilot remains auditable and production-minded.

AI stack - Google & OpenAI PostgreSQL + pgvector LangSmith traces Google Cloud Mexico

Citizen intake

Conversational registration, skills capture, consent, and baseline risk detection.

Pathway assignment

Talisis learning assets, UNID pathway logic, and employability milestones are selected in one pass.

Tutoring and nudges

Adaptive support keeps learners moving and flags when a human coach should step in.

Policy intelligence

Anonymized progression and demand signals return to a government dashboard for course and budget decisions.

Agent 01

Skills Diagnostic

Builds a structured profile from citizen conversation, prior learning, and self-reported experience; outputs a normalized skills vector and confidence score.

Agent 02

Career Path

Matches citizen capability and local employer demand to role clusters, wage trajectories, and placement probability in the target state economy.

Agent 03

Learning Path

Converts the target role into a modular training sequence across Talisis and UNID assets, with prerequisites, pacing, and verification logic.

Agent 04

AI Tutor

Delivers coaching, practice, reminders, and short-form explanation while escalating sensitive cases or confidence failures to human review.

Agent 05

Policy Intelligence

Aggregates anonymized demand, completion, and credential signals to help state governments improve budget allocation and workforce strategy.

Structured data contracts make the system grant-ready and scalable. Each agent can publish a typed output such as learner profile, recommended role cluster, pathway plan, tutoring event, or policy insight. That gives Talisis AI Lab and the technical team a clean path toward multi-agent deployment without rewriting the full experience.

Responsible AI controls

Human-in-the-loop escalation, consent-aware data collection, audit trails, prompt and output logging, quality monitoring through LangSmith, and policy dashboards built only from anonymized aggregates.

Deployment posture

A pragmatic pilot on Google Cloud in Mexico can validate data residency, monitoring, integration points, and government adoption before heavier agent orchestration is turned on.

Pilot roadmap

A practical path from proposal to state deployment

The proposal is stronger when the first year reads like an adoption plan, not just a build plan. These phases keep the system grounded in validation, responsible AI, and government usability.

Phase 1

Prototype validation

Validate the functional architecture with Talisis AI Lab, confirm workflow design with the technical team, and test user journeys for citizens, tutors, and government operators.

Phase 2

Quintana Roo pilot

Launch a government-aligned pilot focused on economic opportunity, beginning with high-fit sectors such as tourism, bilingual service, or adjacent logistics roles.

Phase 3

Scale and replication

Introduce multi-agent specialization, replicate to additional states, and publish reusable operating models, metrics, and governance patterns for wider public-sector adoption.
